THE PROSIDIC FEATURES OF SPEECH

Welcome to this Website. It's all about the speech and its Features.

By Group Houdini on May 8, 2018

A. PAUSE

  • A temporary stop.
  • Can be indicated in writing through full stops, colons, semi-colons and commas.

B. PITCH (INOTATION)

  • Patterns of rise and fall.
  • Portrays emotions.
  1. Inlift - the speaker raises the pitch of the voice in an interrogative way in the middle of a sentence, as if seeking confirmation of the listenerâ€™s comprehension.

C. VOLUME

  • Softness or loudness to indicate stress.
  • Used to show emotions.
  • Can be indicated in writing through exclamation points, or typographically with capitals or italics (or both).

D. STRESS

  • A stressed word or syllable is usually preceded by a very slight pause, and is spoken at slightly increased volume.

RULES:

  1. Forsyllables
    • Compound Nouns: first word is stressed.
    • homographs: Noun- first syllable is stressed; verb- second syllable is stressed.
  2. For words
    • Content Words- stressed.
    • Function Words- unstressed.

E. TEMPO

  • Pace (slowness or fastness).
